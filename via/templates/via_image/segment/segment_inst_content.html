What to Draw

Please draw shapes around objects that pertrain to driving scene and correspond to the labels outlined in labels.txt. The goal is to generate segmentations that are as fine grained as possible for visual prediction tasks on urban street scenes. Some example categories include:


Cars

Pedestrians

Traffic Lights and Traffic Signs


Please make the region as tight as possible. Your shape should exactly follow the material boundary. Use the "Adjust" button to improve the shape boundary, after you have drawn the shape.

If there are objects with holes in them (i.e fences), make sure to only label the foreground objects. More detailed labeling protocol information can be found at The Cityscapes Dataset Website. We follow a similar labeling policy here.

The sections below will outline how to use the tool and provide some examples, good and bad, as guidlines for effective segmentation of images

Once you have drawn around all distinct objects in the image, click the "Submit" and follow the instructions to proceed.


How to draw

  • 1 Click on the object boundary

  • 2Click along the object boundary

  • 3 Click Close when finished

Adjusting your shapes

After drawing a polygon, please adjust its edges so that it better fits the true boundary. When you are done adjusting, switch back to "Draw" mode to draw more polygons.

  • 1 Switch to "Adjust" mode.

  • 2 Click on the polygon.

  • 3 Drag the small white circles.

Add Labels

Please add the labels first before segmenting the image.

To close the shape, you do not need to click near the first point. You can can click "Close" to connect the first and last point.

Zoom in to see better

Scroll with the mouse wheel to zoom in. Click "Reset Zoom" or press escape to reset back to normal.


Move around in the image

Press 'space' and drag with the mouse to move around in the image (panning). You can also move press the arrow keys to move.


Other controls

Undo
If you make a mistake, click "Undo"
Redo
Redo and action previously undone
Close
Click the Close button to close a polygon
Delete
You can delete polygons by selecting them with the "Adjust" button and pressing Delete.
Reset Zoom
When zoomed in, you you can click "Reset Zoom" to zoom out again.

Why are we doing this?

We want to build a scalable platform for fine-grained annotation of urban scenes and demonstrate its effectiveness by collecting a large-scale database of driving scenes. By collecting these images and their annotations we seek to advance the state of computer vision in autonomous driving and alleviate the lack of supervised data in this domain
